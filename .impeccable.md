# Movie Scrubber — Design Context

## Users

Bay Area cinephiles checking what is playing today at their local arthouse and
chain theaters (Alamo Drafthouse Mountain View / Valley Fair, AMC Sunnyvale,
AMC Mercado, eventually more). They scan the page in five to ninety seconds —
on a phone over coffee, or on a laptop late afternoon when they're picking
something to see that night. The job to be done is "what's on, where, and when
— help me decide quickly." It is not a transactional ticketing app. We do not
sell tickets, take reservations, or run promotions. The page is a curated
listing.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **considered**, **literate**, **calm**.

Voice closer to *Film Comment* or the Criterion Channel daily program than to
Fandango or the AMC app. Confident editorial typography. Whitespace as a
deliberate signal of restraint, not a hole to fill with chrome. Tone is
attentive — the listing was assembled by a person who cares about cinema, not
by a marketing engine.

## Aesthetic Direction

Editorial print listing — the back of a Sunday Arts section, or the front
matter of a film festival booklet, transposed into the browser. Warm
off-white paper. Warm near-black ink. One signal accent: vermilion (the color
of a paper ticket stub and a marquee bulb). Used twice on the page, never
more.

**References (positive):** Criterion Channel daily listings, *Sight & Sound*
magazine inside pages, A24 zines, the New York Times Sunday Arts movie grid,
old Pacific Film Archive calendars.

**Anti-references (do not look like):** Fandango, Atom Tickets, the AMC app,
glassmorphism dashboard demos, Apple Liquid Glass marketing pages, Notion
"clean" SaaS look, anything with frosted blur layered on a synthetic
gradient.

**Theme:** light, warm. The product is browsed in daylight or warm domestic
evening light, not on a trading desk. Dark mode is intentionally not
provided.

## Design Principles

1. **Type does the work.** A condensed display sans for movie titles and a
   refined serif for everything else. No icons. No badges. No pills. Hierarchy
   is created with size, weight, and italic — not with containers.

2. **Negative space over containers.** Movies are sections separated by
   whitespace and a 1px hairline rule. There are no cards. There is no glass.
   There are no nested rounded rectangles. If a thing needs visual separation,
   the space around it provides it.

3. **The grid asymmetric, the rhythm regular.** Content sits in a
   left-indented column, not centered. Vertical rhythm between movies is
   consistent and generous. Showtimes right-align so the eye can scan them
   like a TV grid.

4. **One accent, used twice.** Vermilion appears on the masthead date marker
   and as a single small dot beside the next upcoming showtime. Nowhere else.

5. **Past is muted, present is ink, future is ink.** A showtime that has
   already started fades to the muted ink color. We do not strike it through.

## Type & Color Tokens

- Display: **Big Shoulders Display** (700 / 800), condensed editorial sans —
  evokes marquee signage; not in the model's reflex font list.
- Body / showtimes: **Spectral** (300 / 400 italic / 500), Production Type
  serif designed for screen reading; not in the model's reflex font list.
- Numerals: Spectral with `font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums lining-nums`.

OKLCH palette (warm parchment, neutrals tinted toward 70°):

```
--paper:    oklch(97% 0.012 75)   /* warm off-white */
--ink:      oklch(22% 0.015 70)   /* warm near-black */
--ink-soft: oklch(45% 0.015 70)   /* secondary */
--ink-mute: oklch(60% 0.010 70)   /* past showtimes, captions */
--rule:     oklch(82% 0.012 70)   /* hairlines */
--accent:   oklch(56% 0.18 28)    /* vermilion ticket stub */
```

## Anti-Patterns Banned in This Project

- Glassmorphism (no `backdrop-filter`, no translucent stacked panels)
- Cards inside cards
- Side-stripe borders (`border-left`/`border-right` > 1px)
- Gradient text (`background-clip: text` + gradient)
- Pills, chips, badges for tags or showtimes
- Pure `#000` or pure `#fff`
- Drop shadows on rounded rectangles
- Centered-everything layouts
- More than one accent color
